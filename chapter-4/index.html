Chapter IV: Liquidity and Efficiency
@ Pika Investor | Saturday, Nov 14, 2020 | 1 minute read | Update at Saturday, Nov 14, 2020

Liquidity and debt ratios indicate how capable a business is in meeting its short term obligations as well protecting the long term health of its business. Efficiency ratios measure its ability to use what it has to generate profits. It can also reflect how effective the management is.

Some examples of these ratios: a. current ratio b. quick ratio c. debt to equity ratio d. interest coverage ratio e. asset turnover ratio f. inventory turnover ratio
