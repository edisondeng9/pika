Chapter VI: Paralysis by Analysis
@ Pika Investor | Monday, Nov 16, 2020 | 2 minute read | Update at Monday, Nov 16, 2020

Analysis paralysis describes an individual process when overanalysizing or overthinking a situation can cause forward motion or decision making to become paralyzed, meaning that no solution or course of action is decided upon. The keyword here is: Less is more.

There are 3 kinds of investing strategies: growth investing, dividend investing and value investing.

The ratios vary from industry to industry hence it is important to compare ratios for companies within the same industry. The same concept to be apply on the investing strategies. Certain ratios imply higher weightage in certain strategies.

Growth investing: these companies instead of paying dividend, they reinvest the profits back into the business for growth. Some criterion to look out include high PE ratio and consistent increase in EPS.

Want to seat back and collect regular payout? Dividend investing is the one for you. Planting money trees, as it suggests, investors love dividend investing as there is dividend income from regular payout and capital appreciation as prices of stocks tend to go up over time. Usually matured companies as they have strong financials to pay out regularly and tend to be more expensive and have lower potential for growth.

Value investing aka contrarian investing, tends to buy the underdog shares. Some investors will think it is a bad stock and sell-offs while value investors see this as a buying opportunity at discounted rate. However, value investing might require a longer time horizon as investors have to wait for them to appreciate in value. These stocks might also be a downer and never recover.
